Heritage

Banaue Rice Terraces

Ifugao, Cordillera Region

2,000-year-old terraces carved into the mountains, often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World". A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Beach

Pagudpud Beach

Ilocos Norte, Region I

Known as the "Boracay of the North" with pristine white sand and world-class kitesurfing conditions.

Culture

Vigan Heritage City

Ilocos Sur, Region I

Spanish colonial town with cobblestone streets and ancestral houses. A beautifully preserved UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Beach

La Union

San Juan, La Union

"Elyu" is the popular, casual nickname for the province of La Union in the Philippines, derived from the initials "L" and "U". Renowned as the "Surfing Capital of the North," it is a premier weekend destination for surfing, vibrant nightlife, beach parties, and popular cafes, particularly around San Juan.

Cave

Sumaguing Cave

Sagada, Mountain Province

is the largest ("Big Cave") and most popular, offering dramatic, high-definition views of towering limestone formations,, stalactites, and stalagmites

Must-Try Luzon Delicacies

Food

Pancit Bato

Camarines Norte, Bicol

Thick rice noodles stir-fried with vegetables, meat, and special sauce. A regional specialty you won't find elsewhere.

Food

Bagnet

Ilocos Region

Deep-fried crispy pork belly that's perfectly crunchy on the outside. Best served with KBL (kamatis, bagoong, lasona).

Food

Pinikpikan

Cordillera Region

Traditional Cordillera chicken soup with etag (smoked pork) and vegetables. A ceremonial dish with deep cultural significance.

Food

Ilocos Empanada

Ilocos Region

Orange-colored meat pastry filled with ground meat, egg, and vegetables. A beloved street food with a distinctive crispy shell.

Food

Sisig

Pampanga

made from chopped pork (traditionally pig face/ears, often mixed with belly), chicken liver, onions, chili peppers, and calamansi, frequently served on a hot sizzling plate
